# About Mathemalchemy

**Mathemalchemy** is a large, immersive art installation celebrating the creativity and beauty of mathematics, built collaboratively by [[The Team|24 mathematical artists and artistic mathematicians]]. It is a whimsical miniature world — a bay, a bakery, a garden, a lighthouse — where every element embodies real mathematics, from [[Zeno's Paradoxes and Limits|Zeno's paradox]] to [[Knot Theory|knot theory]] to the [[Fractals and Self-Similarity|Mandelbrot set]].

## Mission
To showcase mathematics as creative, beautiful, and welcoming — "a garden of mathematical delights... for everyone" — and to demonstrate what mathematicians and artists can build together.

## Origin story
- **Fall 2019** — Mathematician **Ingrid Daubechies** (Duke) saw fiber artist **Dominique Ehrmann**'s quilted installation *Time to Break Free* (2019) — a fantastical machine converting the flat figures of a traditional quilt into fully three-dimensional characters — and wondered whether something similar could bring the beauty and creativity of mathematical ideas to life.
- **January 2020** — Daubechies and Ehrmann launched the project at the Joint Mathematics Meetings in Denver; fourteen artists committed on the spot. The team grew to twenty-four.
- **2020–2021** — Built remotely through the COVID-19 pandemic, coordinating by Zoom workshops.
- **July 2021** — Three-week in-person assembly at Duke University's Rhodes Information Initiative.
- **January 2022** — Began touring (see [[Exhibition History]]).

## Cast of characters within the Installation
The installation is populated by animal characters, most named for mathematicians or mathematical objects, and three human figures, whether they are observing, inside or imagining the Mathemalchemy world into existence is a question left to open.

- **Tess the Tortoise** (knitted, ceramic shell) walks Zeno's Path daily → [[Tess the Tortoise]]
- **Arnold** the baker cat (for Vladimir Arnold) and his assistant **Mo[u]se** (for Jürgen Moser — together, the KAM theorem) → [[Mandelbrot Bakery]]
- **Harriet** the bowerbird (for the Harriss spiral) runs "Conway's Curios"; her brother **Horton** (Conway's middle name) runs the terrace café → [[The Curio Shop]]
- **Chipmunks Uinta and Durango** play a [[Prime Numbers|prime-number]] game on the playground; **squirrels** run the Sieve of Eratosthenes in the [[The Garden and Reef|garden]]
- **Puffins Del and Nabla** (∇) tend [[The Lighthouse]]
- **Herons** tag-and-release knots in the bay; **penguins Adélie, Chinstrap, and Gentoo** are visiting knot theorists → [[Knotical (Knotilus Bay)]]
- An octopus ("OctoPi") paints [[The Mural]]

**The three silhouettes** - each female, perhaps a nod to the fact that 20 out of 24 creators of the project are women:
* A **child** improvising on a trumpet - the swirls of her music seed the scene's vortices
* A **teenager** surfing a rocket skateboard, carrying a pennant flag with a hidden message
* An **adult** releasing a cavalcade of mathematical notes into the world

The characters star in the official **comic book**: [A Comic Book Adventure in Math and Art](https://mathemalchemy.org/a-comic-book-adventure-in-math-and-art/). Many also appear in the team's own AMS Notices article, "Ars Mathemalchemica" (Aug 2022, [DOI 10.1090/noti2515](https://doi.org/10.1090/noti2515)), told as a dialogue between Harriet and Arnold.
