# About Mathemalchemy

**Mathemalchemy** is a large, immersive art installation celebrating the creativity and beauty of mathematics, built collaboratively by [[The Team|24 mathematical artists and artistic mathematicians]]. It is a whimsical miniature world — a bay, a bakery, a garden, a lighthouse — where every element embodies real mathematics, from [[Zeno's Paradoxes and Limits|Zeno's paradox]] to [[Knot Theory|knot theory]] to the [[Fractals and Self-Similarity|Mandelbrot set]].

## Origin story
- **Fall 2019** — Mathematician **Ingrid Daubechies** (Duke) saw fiber artist **Dominique Ehrmann**'s installation at Cape Cod and envisioned a collaboration with the mathematical art community.
- **January 2020** — Daubechies and Ehrmann launched the project at the Joint Mathematics Meetings in Denver; fourteen artists committed on the spot. The team grew to twenty-four.
- **2020–2021** — Built remotely through the COVID-19 pandemic, coordinating by Zoom workshops.
- **July 2021** — Three-week in-person assembly at Duke University's Rhodes Information Initiative.
- **January 2022** — Began touring (see [[Exhibition History]]).

## By the numbers (as of Sept 2021)
- ~396 hours on video calls
- 8,326 emails exchanged
- ~1,063 people-hours of assembly and fabrication
- Funding support included a Leverhulme Trust Grant via **Dorothy Buck** (Duke)

## Mission
To showcase mathematics as creative, beautiful, and welcoming — "a garden of mathematical delights... for everyone" — and to demonstrate what mathematicians and artists can build together.

## Cast of characters
The installation is populated by animal characters: **Tess the Tortoise** (knitted, with ceramic shell — see [[Tess the Tortoise]]), chipmunks who sort [[Prime Numbers|primes]] in the [[The Garden and Reef|garden]], an octopus ("OctoPi") who paints [[The Mural]], and more. The characters star in the official **comic book**: [A Comic Book Adventure in Math and Art](https://mathemalchemy.org/a-comic-book-adventure-in-math-and-art/).
