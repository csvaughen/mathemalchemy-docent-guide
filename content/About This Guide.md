# About This Guide

This is an **unofficial companion guide** for docents, students, instructors and visitors, prepared by the Montgomery County Community College (MCCC) volunteers for Mathemalchemy's 2026 Philadelphia-area visit. It is a study aid, not an official publication of the Mathemalchemy team.

## Credits & sources
- **[Mathemalchemy](https://mathemalchemy.org)** — the installation and all its underlying mathematics and artistry belong to the project's team of 24 mathematicians and artists, co-founded by Ingrid Daubechies and Dominique Ehrmann. The [Mathematical Connections](https://mathemalchemy.org/mathematical-connections/) pages are the primary source for scene content.
- **"Ars Mathemalchemica: From Math to Art and Back Again"** — Susan Goldstine, Elizabeth Paley, and Henry Segerman, *Notices of the American Mathematical Society*, Vol. 69, No. 7 (August 2022), pp. 1220–1228, [doi:10.1090/noti2515](https://doi.org/10.1090/noti2515). Written by three members of the team, this article is the source for much of the history, character lore, and scene detail in this guide — and it tours the installation as a dialogue between Harriet the bowerbird and Arnold the cat, well worth reading in its own right.
- **EntreEd Docent Guide** — the guided-discovery approach here (the three questions, the scene-by-scene activity format) is adapted from a docent guide produced by EntreEd, itself built on the official Mathemalchemy materials.
- Text on this site is our own adaptation and paraphrase, written for docent preparation and classroom outreach.

## Verification status
Scene creator attributions and some details are **still being verified** against the official Mathemalchemy site and the physical installation. Notes carry a `verified-against-official-site` flag in their metadata. If you spot an error, please let a docent coordinator know — corrections are welcome.
