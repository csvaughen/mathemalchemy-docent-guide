# About This Guide

This is an **unofficial companion guide** for docents, students, and visitors, prepared by the Montgomery County Community College (MCCC) hosting volunteers for Mathemalchemy's 2026 Philadelphia-area visit. It is a study aid, not an official publication of the Mathemalchemy team.

## Credits & sources
- **[Mathemalchemy](https://mathemalchemy.org)** — the installation and all its underlying mathematics and artistry belong to the project's team of 24 mathematicians and artists, co-founded by Ingrid Daubechies and Dominique Ehrmann. The [Mathematical Connections](https://mathemalchemy.org/mathematical-connections/) pages are the primary source for scene content.
- **EntreEd Docent Guide** — the docent method here (the three questions, the scene-by-scene activity format) is adapted from a docent guide produced by EntreEd, itself built on the official Mathemalchemy materials.
- Text on this site is our own adaptation and paraphrase, written for docent training and classroom outreach.

## Verification status
Scene creator attributions and some details are **still being verified** against the official Mathemalchemy site and the physical installation. Notes carry a `verified-against-official-site` flag in their metadata. If you spot an error, please let a docent coordinator know — corrections are welcome.

## How to use this site
1. Read [[About Mathemalchemy]] for the story.
2. Learn the method in [[Docent Guide - How to Use]].
3. Pick a route from [[Tour Planning]] and study its scenes.
4. Practice one activity from the [[Activities Index]] before your first tour.

Every page is printable — use your browser's Print function for a clean handout of any scene.
