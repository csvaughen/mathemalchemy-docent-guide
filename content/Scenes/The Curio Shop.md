# The Curio Shop

**Math:** [[Polyhedra and Euler's Formula]] · mathematical artifacts
**Creators (per EntreEd guide):** curated by Susan Goldstine and Henry Segerman, items contributed by the whole team
**Official page:** https://mathemalchemy.org/2022/01/11/math-connections-in-the-curio-shop/

## What it is
A fine-math gallery — **"Conway's Curios"** — of mathematical gems: **Johnson solids**, mechanical computing devices (including an **Antikythera mechanism** homage), and other physical proofs — geometry's rare-object cabinet.

## Lore (AMS Notices, Aug 2022)
- The proprietor is **Harriet, a bowerbird** who "channeled her propensity for collecting mathematically intriguing objects into a thriving business." She's named for the **Harriss spiral** (discovered by team member Edmund Harriss) — the spiral adorns her feathers and the shop sign and wall.
- The shop is named after **John H. Conway, with his family's permission** — like Conway's office, it's full of mathematical curiosities. Harriet's brother **Horton** (Conway's middle name) runs the **terrace café** next door, reached via the lighthouse ramp.
- Inventory called out in the article: Klein bottles, interlocking sliced tori, rhombicosidodecahedra, double helices, fractal trees, Borromean rings — "*objets de mathématique*."
- **Window display:** an **Alexander horned sphere** hung next to the **Möbius strip**, opposite the **Hopf fibration of the three-sphere**. *(This window is almost certainly what the ICM 2026 "hidden gems" blurb garbled into "sourdough Möbius strips" at the bakery.)*
- Harriet's arc in the article — a bowerbird told as a fledgling she had a "little bird brain" for art, rediscovering her artistic confidence — mirrors people who believe math "isn't for them." Great Engage beat for adult visitors.

## Key vocabulary
- **Mathematical artifact** — a physical object demonstrating a mathematical principle.
- **Johnson solid** — a strictly convex polyhedron whose faces are all regular polygons (excluding the uniform ones); exactly 92 exist.
- **Mechanism** — a device like the Antikythera mechanism that computed astronomical positions mechanically.

## Activity: "The Polyhedra Auction"
Describe a solid without naming it ("8 corners, 6 identical faces") — students bid by shouting the name (Cube!). Escalate with **Euler's formula** V − E + F = 2: "4 corners, 4 faces — how many edges am I holding?" (6 — tetrahedron!). Chemists play this game with molecular shapes.

**Takeaway:** all solids obey the same shape laws — the DNA of crystals and architecture.
