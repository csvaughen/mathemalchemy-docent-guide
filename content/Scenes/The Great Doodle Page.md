# The Great Doodle Page

**Math:** [[Axioms, Proofs, and Mathematical Intuition]]
**Theme:** celebrating women mathematicians — Maryam Mirzakhani, Emmy Noether
**Creators (per EntreEd guide):** concept by Ingrid Daubechies; fabrication by Dominique Ehrmann and Kim Whittemore
**Official page:** https://mathemalchemy.org/2022/01/11/math-connections-in-the-great-doodle-page/

## What it is
A quilted "doodle page" visualizing the *thinking process* of great mathematicians — the geometric sketches Mirzakhani famously drew on huge sheets of paper on her floor. It argues that messy drawing is rigorous discovery in progress.

## Docent questions
- "How do these chaotic doodles function as a *visual proof* — a first step toward rigorous discovery?"
- "If your notebook is full of sketches, what does this exhibit say about your brain's spatial reasoning?"
- "In a world of AI-generated answers, why is the human doodle — the intuitive spark — hard to replicate?"

## Key vocabulary
- **Mathematical intuition** — the creative spark used to solve problems before writing a formal proof.
- **Visual proof** — a diagram that shows *why* something is true without words or symbols.
- **Geometric sketching** — drawing shapes to visualize spatial relationships.

## Activity: "Air-Drafting Challenge"
Eyes closed: imagine a cube floating in front of you. Mentally slice it from a top corner to the opposite bottom corner. Feel the new surface; open eyes and draw the cross-section in the air. Mental doodling explores 3D shapes no chalkboard can capture.

**Takeaway:** doodling is a high-level thinking tool, not a distraction.
