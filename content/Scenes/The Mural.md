# The Mural (OctoPi's Mural)

**Math:** [[Projective Geometry and Perspective]]
**Creators (per EntreEd guide):** Dominique Ehrmann, with design input from the whole 24-member collective
**Official page:** https://mathemalchemy.org/2022/01/11/math-connections-in-the-mural/

## What it is
A large perspective-driven mural — painted, in the exhibit's story, by **OctoPi the octopus** — that integrates the exhibit's themes into one landscape and tricks the eye into seeing 3D depth on a flat wall.

## Docent questions
- "Where must you stand to make this flat painting look like a world you could walk into?"
- "Your eyes are being 'hacked' by projective geometry — can you find the vanishing point?"
- "This is the exact math of VR headsets and 3D game engines — how?"

## Key vocabulary
- **Projective geometry** — properties that survive projection onto a surface.
- **Vanishing point** — where parallel lines appear to meet in a perspective drawing.
- **Perspective** — the technique that creates the illusion of depth.

## Activity: "Perspective Pinch"
Look down the long axis of the gallery; pinch the air where the top and bottom of the far wall appear to meet. Fingers nearly touch though the wall is ten feet tall — your brain performing a live linear projection, the same math as every 3D game engine.

**Takeaway:** perspective is a mathematical hack of the eye — the foundation of VR.
