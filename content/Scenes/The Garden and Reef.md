# The Garden and Reef

**Math:** [[Hyperbolic Geometry]] · [[Fractals and Self-Similarity]] · [[Prime Numbers]]
**Creators (per EntreEd guide):** Faye Goldman (origami), Li-Mei Lim, and the crochet team including Daina Taimina
**Official page:** https://mathemalchemy.org/2022/01/11/math-connections-in-the-garden/

## What it is
A garden and coral reef of **hyperbolic crochet** and folded paper — surfaces that ruffle and curve because they carry more area than flat space allows. Daina Taimina literally invented hyperbolic crochet, and this scene descends from that tradition. Meanwhile, **chipmunks sort prime numbers** in the garden — a number-theory Easter egg visitors love.

## Docent questions
- "Why does this crochet coral ruffle and curve at the edges instead of lying flat?"
- "What if I told you you're looking at *non-Euclidean* space?"
- "Coral packs huge surface area into small space to filter food — what could engineers copy from that?" (biomimicry: filters, carbon scrubbers)

## Key vocabulary
- **Hyperbolic geometry** — space that curves away from itself like a kale leaf; parallel lines *diverge*.
- **Fractal growth** — small parts resembling the whole.
- **Riemann surface** — a complex-analysis surface, visualized in the garden's "cliffs."

## Activity: "Parallel Divergence Walk"
Two students walk side by side, one foot apart. In flat space they'd stay parallel forever. On the "hyperbolic reef," each forward step adds a half-step apart — the gap grows exponentially. That's negative curvature, nature's trick for cramming surface area into coral and kale.

**Takeaway:** nature is a hyperbolic engineer.
