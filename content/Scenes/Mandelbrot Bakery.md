# Mandelbrot Bakery

**Math:** [[Fractals and Self-Similarity]]
**Creators (per EntreEd guide):** fabricated by Kim Whittemore and Dominique Ehrmann
**Official page:** https://mathemalchemy.org/2022/01/05/math-connections-bakery/

## What it is
A bakery whose baked goods and décor follow the recursive boundary of the **Mandelbrot set** — dough "kneaded" by iterating a simple rule (z → z² + c) millions of times.

## Docent questions
- "If you bit into a Mandelbrot cookie, why would the inside look exactly like the outside?"
- "What if I told you this is how your phone compresses photos?"
- "What simple step, repeated, could make something infinitely detailed?"

## Key vocabulary
- **Mandelbrot set** — complex numbers whose iteration stays bounded; its boundary is infinitely intricate and self-similar.
- **Iteration** — repeatedly applying a function, like kneading dough.
- **Self-similarity** — parts resembling the whole at every zoom level.

## Activity: "Action Iteration Loop"
Seed rule: *"Clap, then turn 90°."* Iterate four times — you've traced a square. Add a constant: *"Clap, step forward, turn 90°"* — now iteration traces a moving spiral. The Mandelbrot set is nothing but a seed rule (z² + c) iterated millions of times.

**Takeaway:** iteration is the universe's recipe — and the trick behind fractal compression of images and video.
