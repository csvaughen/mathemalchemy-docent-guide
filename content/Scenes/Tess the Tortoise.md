# Tess the Tortoise

**Math:** [[Zeno's Paradoxes and Limits]] · [[Infinite Series and Convergence]]
**Creators (per EntreEd guide):** Dominique Ehrmann (lead fabricator) and the Mathemalchemy team — knitted tortoise with ceramic shell
**Official page:** https://mathemalchemy.org/2022/01/11/math-connections-tortoise/

## What it is
Tess is the exhibit's beloved mascot — a knitted tortoise with a ceramic shell, journeying along **Zeno's Path**, a quilted trail whose segments shrink as she approaches her destination. She embodies Zeno's paradox of motion: to arrive, first cover half the distance, then half the remainder, forever.

## Docent questions
- "If Tess always has to travel half the remaining distance, will she ever truly arrive — or walk forever?"
- "Look at the quilted segments on Zeno's Path — what do you notice about their size closer to the destination?"
- "Can you think of a real-life situation where you get closer and closer to something but never quite reach it?"

## Key vocabulary
- **Zeno's Paradox** — motion seems impossible because reaching a goal requires infinitely many halfway steps.
- **Infinite sequence** — an endless list of numbers following a rule.
- **Convergence** — when an infinite series adds up to a specific finite total: ½ + ¼ + ⅛ + … = 1.

## Activity: "The Asymptotic Approach"
Pick a line on the floor; the group stands a few feet back. Repeat the instruction: *"Take a step covering exactly half the remaining distance."* Steps become tiny shuffles — the group converges on the line without crossing it. The **limit** of the journey is exactly that line.

**Takeaway:** infinity can be tamed by a limit — infinitely many small steps can still lead to a definite end.
