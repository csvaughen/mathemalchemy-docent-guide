## What it is
A terraced landscape that visualizes the **Lebesgue integral**: measuring a shape by slicing it *horizontally* (terrace by terrace) instead of the familiar vertical Riemann rectangles from Calc I.

## Docent questions
- "Does this look more like a staircase or a natural landscape?"
- "Here's a better way to measure the 'unmeasurable' — what changes if you slice sideways instead of up-and-down?"
- "MRI scans and glacier-volume measurements slice this way — why?"

## Key vocabulary
- **Measure theory** — assigning size/volume to complicated sets of points.
- **Lebesgue integral** — integration by horizontal slices (function values) rather than vertical ones (domain).
- **Approximation** — estimating complex volumes with simpler shapes.

## Activity: "Rising Tide Measurement"
Everyone stands in an imaginary dry pool. Call rising "water levels" — knees! waist! shoulders! — freezing everyone below each line. You've measured the group layer-by-layer (horizontal, Lebesgue) rather than person-by-person (vertical, Riemann).

**Takeaway:** slicing the world horizontally is often the smartest way to measure complexity.
