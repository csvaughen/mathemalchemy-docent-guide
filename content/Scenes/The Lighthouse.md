# The Lighthouse

**Math:** [[Trigonometry and Navigation]]
**Creators (per EntreEd guide):** Henry Segerman and the 3D-fabrication group
**Official page:** https://mathemalchemy.org/2022/01/11/math-connections-lighthouse/

## What it is
The bay's lighthouse — a focal point for **spherical coordinates**, triangulation, and how light propagates: the geometry of finding your way on a curved planet.

## Docent questions
- "How does the lighthouse use angles to reach a ship far away on a curved ocean?"
- "Did you know you're seeing how we navigate by the stars — and how GPS works?"
- "How is beaming laser data between satellites the same math?"

## Key vocabulary
- **Trigonometry** — relationships between sides and angles of triangles.
- **Spherical coordinates** — 3D "GPS addresses" for points on a sphere.
- **Wave propagation** — how light or sound travels through space over time.

## Activity: "The Triangulation Pointer"
Pick a target "ship" in the room. Two students in different corners are "lighthouses," each pointing an arm at it. Knowing the distance between the lighthouses and the two arm angles, sines and cosines pin the ship's exact position. That's GPS, 2,000 years early.

**Takeaway:** triangles are the strongest shape in construction *and* navigation.
