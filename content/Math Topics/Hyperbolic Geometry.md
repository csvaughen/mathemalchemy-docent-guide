# Hyperbolic Geometry

**In the exhibit:** [[The Garden and Reef]] (crochet corals, kale-like ruffles)

The non-Euclidean geometry of **negative curvature**: space curves away from itself, parallel lines diverge, and triangles have angle sums below 180°. Impossible to embed flatly — which is why hyperbolic surfaces *ruffle*. **Daina Taimina** (team member!) invented hyperbolic crochet at Cornell in 1997, finally giving mathematicians a durable physical model after a century of failed paper ones.

Nature uses it constantly: corals, kelp, kale, sea slugs — organisms maximizing surface area in limited space.

**One-liner for tours:** "That ruffle isn't decoration — it's what space looks like when parallel lines refuse to stay parallel."
