# Fluid Dynamics and Vortices

**In the exhibit:** [[Silhouettes and Vortices]]

The mathematics of how liquids and gases move — governed by the Navier–Stokes equations (whose full understanding is literally a $1M Millennium Prize problem). When flow passes an obstacle, it sheds alternating swirls: a **von Kármán vortex street**, visible in clouds behind islands, flags flapping, and the hum of power lines.

**Turbulence** — flow too chaotic for exact prediction — connects to chaos theory and drives weather/climate modeling, aircraft design, and storm forecasting.

**One-liner for tours:** "This is the math behind a hurricane — and behind why flags flap."
