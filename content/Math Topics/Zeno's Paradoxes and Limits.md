# Zeno's Paradoxes and Limits

**In the exhibit:** [[Tess the Tortoise]] (Zeno's Path)

Zeno of Elea (5th c. BCE) argued motion is impossible: to reach a goal you must first go halfway, then half the remainder, forever — infinitely many steps. The resolution took two millennia: the concept of a **limit**. The infinite sum ½ + ¼ + ⅛ + … *converges* to exactly 1. Infinitely many steps, finite total.

**One-liner for tours:** "Infinity can be tamed by a limit — an endless process can still have a definite destination."

**Classroom tie-in (MCCC):** the opening idea of Calc I (limits) and Calc II (series). Tess is a pre-made hook for both. See also [[Infinite Series and Convergence]].
