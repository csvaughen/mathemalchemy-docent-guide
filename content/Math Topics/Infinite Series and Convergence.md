# Infinite Series and Convergence

**In the exhibit:** [[Ball Arches]] (temari balls as series terms) · [[Tess the Tortoise]] (geometric series)

A **series** adds the terms of an infinite sequence. It **converges** when partial sums approach a finite limit — e.g., the geometric series Σ rⁿ converges to 1/(1−r) for |r| < 1. The Ball Arches make the terms physical: each shrinking ball is a term, and the whole arch tops out at a finite height.

**One-liner for tours:** "You are looking at an infinite sum equaling a finite number."
