# Knot Theory

**In the exhibit:** [[Knotical (Knotilus Bay)]]

A branch of [[Topology]] studying **mathematical knots** — closed loops in 3D space. Two knots are equivalent if one deforms into the other without cutting. **Invariants** (crossing number, tricolorability, Jones polynomial…) stay fixed under deformation and let us prove two knots are genuinely different.

Real-world stakes: DNA gets knotted; **topoisomerase** enzymes cut and reseal strands so cells can replicate — and some antibiotics and chemotherapies work by sabotaging exactly this. Team members Dorothy Buck and Samantha Pezzimenti research knots professionally. → [[The Team]]

The biology here is rich enough for its own outreach thread — topoisomerases, drug targets, DNA nanotechnology, and lab techniques. See the **Biology & biotech connections** section of [[Knotical (Knotilus Bay)]] for MCCC faculty/student hooks.

**One-liner for tours:** "Your life depends on knot math — your cells untangle DNA billions of times a day."
