# Dynamical Systems

**In the exhibit:** [[The Terrace]] (Baker's map, traveling waves, Poincaré's telescope) · echoed in [[Mandelbrot Bakery]] (Arnold's cat map, the KAM theorem)

A **dynamical system** is just a rule for how a state changes step by step, or moment by moment — and the same handful of rules show up everywhere from kneading dough to predicting orbits. The **Baker's map** stretches a shape and folds it back on itself, like kneading; iterate it and nearby points that started close together end up scattered unpredictably — an early, hands-on picture of **chaos**. **Henri Poincaré**, working on the three-body problem in celestial mechanics in the 1890s, was the first to show that even simple, fully determined systems can behave this unpredictably — the discovery that founded the field.

**One-liner for tours:** "Simple rules, followed exactly, can still surprise you — that's chaos."
