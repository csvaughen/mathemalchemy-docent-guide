# Topology

**In the exhibit:** [[Knotical (Knotilus Bay)]] · underpins [[Knot Theory]]

"Rubber-sheet geometry": the study of properties preserved under continuous deformation — stretching and bending allowed, cutting and gluing forbidden. The famous joke: a topologist can't tell a donut from a coffee cup (each has exactly one hole).

Key idea for tours: **invariants** — quantities that never change no matter how you deform the object (number of holes, knot invariants). If two objects differ in an invariant, no amount of stretching turns one into the other.

**One-liner for tours:** "A donut and a coffee mug are mathematically the same — and that idea helps cure genetic disease."
