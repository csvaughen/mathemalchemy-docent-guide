# Tessellations and Wallpaper Groups

**In the exhibit:** [[Quiltlet]] · related: [[Frieze Tables]]

A **tessellation** tiles the plane with no gaps or overlaps. The deep theorem: every periodic 2D pattern belongs to one of exactly **17 wallpaper groups**, classified by their combinations of translation, rotation, reflection, and glide reflection. Proven complete in the 1890s (Fedorov); the Alhambra's artisans found many of them empirically centuries earlier.

**One-liner for tours:** "Every wallpaper, tile floor, and crystal in the universe picks from a menu of just 17 patterns."

**Related:** in one dimension (border strips) the menu shrinks to 7 — see [[Frieze Groups]]. Crystallography uses the 3D analogue (230 space groups).
