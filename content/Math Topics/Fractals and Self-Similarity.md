# Fractals and Self-Similarity

**In the exhibit:** [[Mandelbrot Bakery]] (Mandelbrot set) · [[The Cavalcade]] (Koch snowflake) · [[The Garden and Reef]] (fractal growth)

**Fractals** are shapes with detail at every scale, typically **self-similar** — parts resemble the whole. Built by **iteration/recursion**: apply a simple rule to its own output, repeatedly.

- **Koch snowflake** — infinite perimeter, finite area ([[The Cavalcade]]).
- **Mandelbrot set** — iterate z → z² + c; the boundary between "stays bounded" and "escapes" is infinitely intricate ([[Mandelbrot Bakery]]).
- **Fractal growth** — ferns, coastlines, lungs, coral ([[The Garden and Reef]]).

Applications: image/video compression, antenna design, modeling coastlines and markets.

**One-liner for tours:** "Recursion is nature's copy-paste: a tiny rule, repeated, builds infinite complexity."
