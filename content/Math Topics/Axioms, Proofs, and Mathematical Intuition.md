# Axioms, Proofs, and Mathematical Intuition

**In the exhibit:** [[Stack of Books]] (the formal edifice) · [[The Great Doodle Page]] (the intuitive spark)

These two scenes form a deliberate pair — the two halves of how mathematics actually gets made:

- **Intuition first** ([[The Great Doodle Page]]): the "gut feeling," the doodle, the visual proof. Maryam Mirzakhani drew geometric sketches on giant paper sheets; Emmy Noether reasoned in bold abstractions. Discovery is messy.
- **Rigor second** ([[Stack of Books]]): **axioms** (accepted starting truths) support **theorems** (proven consequences) in an unbroken logical chain running from Euclid to today.
